From the experiments, we can see that US latencies are much lower than EU latencies, as expected. That's a physics problem, where obviously the greater
distance from my computer in Champaign has lower latency when making requests to a VM in the midwest as opposed to a VM somewhere in Europe. 
The other pattern we see is that registration is much faster than listing, because of how firestore handles the operation. Since we provide a key
for registration, firestore can simply write it without doing any further scans or queries. However, in the case of listing, firestore needs to 
technically run a query. This results in increased latency over the register operation. 

The other experiment shows that over 100 iterations of registering users to the US server and listing users from the EU server, we see no 
misses. This shows us that firestore's consistency mechanism can actually keep up with what our script is throwing it. Technically there is no guarantee on consistency in this 
case, but that doesn't mean that the system will necessarily have misses. Either our script or servers are too slow to really test the limits of firestore's consistency mechanism.
